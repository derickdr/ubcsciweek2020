  2. UBC PATHS

    UBC PATHS (Parkinson’s and Alzheimer’s Targeted Hope and Support) is a non-profit community-based club that focuses on raising awareness and funds for common neurodegenerative diseases. We engage the campus through our educational series, enrich our community through fundraisers for the Parkinson's and Alzheimer's Societies of BC, and explore with our students and academic professionals through our case studies and conference. Proceeds are directly donated to the Parkinson Society British Columbia and the Alzheimer Society British Columbia.

    AMS Medical Genetics

    Our club aims to keep students up to date with recent advancements through discussions about current med gen topics and relevant research (especially research done by UBC students/faculty!). We hope to connect interested undergraduates with medgen graduate students so they can better prepare to continue their academic careers and become competitive applicants. We also want to help students identify interesting courses to take, potential research supervisors, and potential graduate programs in which they can continue their genetic studies. Finally, we want to better educate students regarding bioethical issues related to genetic screening, gene therapy and human genetic engineering.
